    • Facts about EARTH

  • 1. Earth is not round, but an oblate spheroid, meaning it's slightly flattened at the poles and bulging at the equator.
  • 2. The planet's core is as hot as the surface of the Sun, with temperatures reaching up to 6,000°C (11,000°F).
  • 3. Earth's magnetic field is weakening, and it's expected to flip in the future, potentially causing widespread electrical failures and communication disruptions.
  • 4. The longest mountain range in the world is not the Himalayas, but the Mid-Ocean Ridge, which stretches for over 65,000 km (40,000 miles) across the ocean floor.
  • 5. Earth has a second moon, called the Moon's "temporary satellite" or "mini-moon," which orbits our planet for a short time before escaping into space.
  • 6. The deepest part of the ocean, the Challenger Deep, is so dark that if you shone a light, it would only illuminate a tiny fraction of the surrounding water.
  • 7. Earth's atmosphere extends far beyond what we can see, with the exosphere reaching out to about 10,000 km (6,200 miles) into space.
  • 8. The planet's rotation is slowing down, which means that days are getting longer by about 1.78 seconds every century.
  • 9. Earth has more than 8,000 active volcanoes, with many more dormant or extinct ones.
  • 10. The planet's surface is constantly moving, with tectonic plates shifting at a rate of about 2-3 cm (0.8-1.2 inches) per year.
